## Hook

Most trading bot videos start with a promise: this bot makes money.

I am starting with the opposite.

I built an AI trading bot, connected it to paper trading, gave it a dashboard, score models, risk gates, crypto tracking, proof reports, and a live readiness vault.

And I am still not letting it trade live.

This video is about why.

## Segment 1: What PMO Is

PMO is my paper trading system.

It watches stocks, ETFs, and crypto. It reads market regime, relative volume, VWAP, opening range behavior, score bands, blocklists, trade history, and journal data.

The goal is not to guess.

The goal is to collect enough evidence that the system can prove when it should trade, when it should wait, and when it should block itself.

On the dashboard, I can see account status, paper proof, open positions, why trades are blocked, score band performance, and whether the system is allowed to do anything.

Right now, live trading is locked.

And that is intentional.

## Segment 2: Why I Do Not Trust Raw Results

The first lesson from building this bot was simple:

Raw results can lie.

At one point, the full journal looked weak. The win rate was not impressive, and profit factor was sitting near break-even.

But when I separated the data, the picture changed.

Some symbols were dragging the system down. Some early opening trades were low-quality. Some high-score trades were not actually better.

That is when the bot started becoming useful.

Not because it found a magic indicator.

Because it found what not to do.

## Segment 3: The Score Inversion Finding

One of the biggest findings was score inversion.

The old assumption was simple: higher score means better trade.

But the journal did not agree.

The clean 65 to 74 score band was stronger than the higher score bands.

That matters because a lot of beginner traders assume confidence equals edge.

In this system, the middle band had better behavior, while the highest scores often needed extra confirmation or were blocked completely.

That changed how I look at the bot.

The score is not permission to trade. It is only one piece of evidence.

## Segment 4: Why the Bot Is Still Paper Only

There are three reasons PMO is still paper only.

First, I need more trades.

A few winners are not enough. A small sample can look amazing and still fall apart. The target is to collect a larger paper-trade sample before trusting any rebuild.

Second, the raw equity track still needs improvement.

Clean filtered results are promising, but live trading needs forward proof under the current rules, not only a post-hoc filtered subset.

Third, I want the system to prove discipline.

Can it sit out on bad days?

Can it block bad symbols?

Can it avoid the first few minutes of the open?

Can it stay locked when the dashboard looks exciting?

That is what I care about.

## Segment 5: What The Dashboard Tracks

The dashboard is built around one idea:

Do not let the bot hide the truth.

It shows paper proof, real journal metrics, data collection progress, open positions, broker reconciliation, Why-Not blockers, score bands, and live readiness.

If something is missing, I want it visible.

If the journal and broker disagree, I want it visible.

If V112 replay history is old data and should not count as current proof, I want it labeled that way.

If the bot is not ready, I do not want a motivational message.

I want the dashboard to say it is not ready.

## Segment 6: What Happens Next

The next goal is simple:

Collect clean paper trades.

The system is tracking toward a larger data target. As trades close, I will audit win rate, profit factor, score bands, symbols, time windows, and exit quality.

If the data supports a rebuild, I will rebuild the score model.

If the data says the edge is not there, I will show that too.

This channel is going to follow that process in public.

Not every video will be a win.

Some will be audits.

Some will be mistakes.

Some will be chart breakdowns.

Some will be crypto tests.

But the point is the same: proof first.
